About The Pineal Gland

The Pineal Gland is a small gland that is located in the center of the brain. It is named for its pine cone-like shape. It sits between the two hemispheres of the brain. It also consists mainly of pinealocytes. These are cells that cells that produce the hormone melatonin. Lastly it has glial cells, which are a particular type of brain cells that support neurons . Cells that transmit information to other cells
