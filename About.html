Common causes of dysfunction

    • A tumor found in the pineal region. Does not begin in the Pineal Gland

    • Another tumor found in the Pineal Gland
  • Parinaud's syndrome
    • The innability to move your eyes up and down
  • Germinoma
    • A germ cell tumor

Hormones Produced

The Pineal Gland produces melatonin hormones that are secreted into the bloodstream Melatonin is responsible for metabolism, sexual development and for regulating other hormones in the body It also helps supports circadian rhythm of the body which represents the day/night cycle important for sleeping and waking up Circadian - An internal 24-hour "clock" that plays a critical role in when we fall asleep and when we wake up.
